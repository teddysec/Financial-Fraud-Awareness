The Current State of E-Banking Fraud in Nigeria

E-Banking Fraud is a fraud executed by using technology to illegally withdraw money from a victim’s bank account and deposit the money to a fraudster’s account in a different bank. The advent of e-banking technology paved the way for limitless banking by enabling bank users to access their bank accounts anywhere and anytime. The e-banking technology has made banking easy for bank users and on the other side, it has made banking regretful for some bank users due to the abuse of the technology from fraudsters. A recent report from the Nigerian Inter-Bank Settlement System (NIBSS) that depicts fraud activities across all banks from January-March 2022 shows that the volume of e-banking fraud increased by 9% with a record of 28,455 successful fraud attempts that led to a total loss of 3.2bn naira across all banks. The mobile and web channels are the most frequent e-banking channels perpetrated to illegally defraud bank users. Other significant channels include Point of Sales (POS) and Automated Teller Machine (ATM). There are various techniques used by fraudsters to exploit customers, the most common technique is social engineering. Don’t freak out, check below for the definition of social engineering. Social engineering is a psychological act used by fraudsters to lure bank users into giving out confidential information which can be used to illegally withdraw money from a bank user’s account. The common types of social engineering attacks are shown below.

Vishing

This is an unsought call from a fraudster acting to be a representative of your bank in order get your confidential information like password, PIN, and OTP. In most cases, fraudsters have your account number.

Smishing

This is a text message from a fraudster with your bank name as sender requesting your action to complete a transaction or resolve a problem.

Phishing

The is a deceitful email from a fraudster pretending to be your bank in order to get your private information. The email address used in this attack is similar to your bank email address.

Skimming

This involves the capturing of the magnetic stripe on your debit card by a fraudster to reproduce a replica which can be used to illegally wwithdraw money from your account.

Pharming

This is used by fraudsters to redirect you to an unsolicited website without your approval. The redirected website is always similar to your bank official website.

Hacking

This is the unauthorized access into your device(s) by a fraudster. Fraudsters can place a keylogger on your device and capture your personal information when you use your device.
