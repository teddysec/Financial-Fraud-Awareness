Detecting E-Banking Fraud

What are the basic red flags?

Fake Text/Call

Fake text or call from a fraudster is a major red flag. Your bank will never text or call you and request for your debit card details. Likewise, your bank will never call or text you to request for One Time Password (OTP).

Email Bait

You received an email requesting you to update your bank details or provide your transaction PIN by clicking a link. This is a common act of fraudsters, neglect such email.

Fake Website

When you browse the internet with your device, you are prone to be redircted to a spoofed website created by a fraudster. Spoofed website are cloned version of your bank website and most cases such website ask for your personal information.

Card Data Theft

Fraudsters can extract your card details when you use it on POS machine. If you notice a strange device on a POS machine, avoid using such machine with your card.

What you need to do

How to protect yourself?

You should do

Be sure to follow it
  • Always keep your card safe
  • Enable two-step verification
  • Use complex passcode
  • Always logout after completing a transaction
  • Change your password regularly
  • Always review of your bank statement for discrepancy
  • Contact your bank if your phone is stolen or misplaced

You should avoid

Be sure to follow it
  • Don't save your bank details on your device(s)
  • Don't share your password with anyone
  • Don't touch your face
  • Avoid handshaking
  • Avoid infected surfaces
  • Avoid droplets
  • Don’t travel

What does complex password involve?

This is the combination of upper case letters, lower case letters, numbers, and special characters. A good example is 4xM&7Lx!
