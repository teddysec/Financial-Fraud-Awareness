What you need to do

How to protect yourself?

You should do

Be sure to follow it
  • Always keep your card safe
  • Enable two-step verification
  • Use complex passcode
  • Always logout after completing a transaction
  • Change your password regularly
  • Always review of your bank statement for discrepancy
  • Contact your bank if your phone is stolen or misplaced

You should avoid

Be sure to follow it
  • Don't save your bank details on your device(s)
  • Don't share your password with anyone
  • Don't reply fake bank text requesting or your details
  • Avoid clicking suspicious link
  • Don't use your personal info as password
  • Avoid unsolicited help from strangers when using ATM
  • Avoid downloading apps from unknown sources

Enable Two-Step Verification

Enable two-step verification to avoid unauthorized access to your bank account.

Update your contact details

In an event of suspiciious activity on your account, your bank will get in touch with as soon as possible.

Keep your card safe

Keep your card safe to avoid the exposure of your card details..

Enable SMS and push alert

Stay updated with your bank security alerts via SMS and mobile app.

What should you do

How to prevent E-Banking Fraud?

Preventing yourself from e-banking fraud starts with you, all it takes is to be security conscious at all times. Stay calm when you receive a call or text from a fraudster, be bold enough to end the conversation or say no. Remember, your bank will never call you and ask for your personal details. Likewise, your bank won't send you an email to access your internet or mobile banking platform.

If you receive a call from an usual phone number format, check the back of your debit card if it tallies. If it does not tally, ignore the call and report the phone number. No one knows your account details better than you, it is your responsibility to stay in control. For every techniques used by fraudsters, there is always an urgent call to action.
